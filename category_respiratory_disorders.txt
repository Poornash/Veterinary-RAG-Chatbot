========================================
CATEGORY: Respiratory Disorders in Dogs & Cats
========================================

This file contains structured, RAG-optimized veterinary summaries of breathing 
problems commonly seen in pets, including symptoms, causes, diagnosis, treatment, 
and emergency signs.

------------------------------------------------------
1. Kennel Cough (Upper Airway Infection)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Dry, hacking cough (“honking cough”)
- Gagging or retching after coughing
- Nasal discharge (mild)
- Occasional fever
- Normal appetite in mild cases

Causes:
- Bordetella bacteria
- Parainfluenza virus
- Spread through contact with infected dogs (boarding, grooming)

Diagnosis:
- Cough history + exposure
- PCR nasal swab if severe

Treatment:
- Cough suppressants (vet prescribed)
- Antibiotics if bacterial infection suspected
- Steam inhalation
- Avoid exercise until recovered

Prevention:
- Bordetella vaccine (oral, intranasal, injectable)
- Avoid crowded dog areas during outbreaks

------------------------------------------------------
2. Pneumonia (Lung Infection)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Wet, productive cough
- Fever
- Difficulty breathing
- Loss of appetite
- Nasal discharge (thick)
- Lethargy

Causes:
- Bacterial infection (common)
- Viral infection
- Aspiration of food/vomit
- Severe untreated kennel cough

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray
- Blood tests
- Tracheal wash (severe cases)

Treatment:
- Strong antibiotics
- Nebulization + coupage therapy
- Oxygen support if needed
- IV fluids

Emergency:
- Blue gums
- Labored breathing
- Collapse

------------------------------------------------------
3. Feline Asthma
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Wheezing sound
- Open-mouth breathing
- Rapid breathing
- Coughing episodes
- Squatting posture during breathlessness

Causes:
- Allergens (dust, smoke, pollen)
- Stress
- Genetics

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray (air trapping in lungs)
- Blood tests
- Exclusion of other diseases

Treatment:
- Inhaled bronchodilators (albuterol)
- Corticosteroids (inhaled or oral)
- Remove dust/allergens (air purifier helps)

Triggers to Avoid:
- Cigarette smoke
- Perfumes/sprays
- Dusty litter

------------------------------------------------------
4. Tracheal Collapse (Small Dogs)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Goose-honking cough
- Worse with excitement or pulling leash
- Breathing difficulty
- Gagging

Breeds Commonly Affected:
- Pomeranian  
- Chihuahua  
- Yorkshire Terrier  
- Shih Tzu  

Causes:
- Weak cartilage rings in windpipe
- Obesity worsens condition

Diagnosis:
- X-ray
- Fluoroscopy
- Endoscopy

Treatment:
- Cough suppressants
- Bronchodilators
- Weight loss
- Harness instead of collar
- Surgery (severe cases)

------------------------------------------------------
5. Feline Upper Respiratory Infection (Cat Flu)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Sneezing
- Runny nose
- Eye discharge
- Fever
- Mouth ulcers (calicivirus)
- Decreased appetite

Causes:
- Feline Herpesvirus (FHV)
- Feline Calicivirus (FCV)
- Secondary bacterial infection

Diagnosis:
- PCR test
- Symptoms + history

Treatment:
- Antibiotics (if bacterial)
- Eye drops (herpes conjunctivitis)
- Steam inhalation
- Nutritious, soft foods
- L-lysine supplements (for FHV support)

Prevention:
- FVRCP vaccination

------------------------------------------------------
6. Bronchitis
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Persistent dry cough
- Wheezing
- Exercise intolerance
- Increased breathing rate

Causes:
- Inflammation of bronchi (usually allergic)
- Infections
- Irritants like smoke

Diagnosis:
- X-ray
- Bronchoscopy (if needed)

Treatment:
- Steroids
- Bronchodilators
- Avoid smoke/dust
- Nebulization therapy

------------------------------------------------------
7. Nasal Mites (Dogs)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Reverse sneezing
- Nasal discharge
- Itchy nose
- Snorting noises

Cause:
- Mites living inside nasal passages

Diagnosis:
- Nasal swab
- Endoscopy

Treatment:
- Ivermectin
- Selamectin

------------------------------------------------------
8. Pleural Effusion (Fluid Around Lungs)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Severe breathing difficulty
- Open-mouth breathing (especially cats)
- Abdominal breathing
- Blue gums (late stage)
- Shallow rapid breaths

Causes:
- Heart disease
- Infection
- Tumors
- FIP in cats

Diagnosis:
- Chest X-ray
- Ultrasound
- Thoracocentesis (fluid removal test)

Treatment:
- Drain fluid (life-saving)
- Treat underlying cause

Emergency:
- ANY difficulty breathing requires IMMEDIATE vet care.
