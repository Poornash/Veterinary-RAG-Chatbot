========================================
CATEGORY: Skin Diseases & Parasite Problems in Pets
========================================

This file contains veterinary-style summaries for dogs and cats 
related to skin conditions, parasites, and dermatological disorders.

------------------------------------------------------
1. Fleas (Ctenocephalides felis)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Intense scratching and biting at skin
- Red bumps, hair loss, scabs
- Black “pepper-like” flea dirt on fur
- Allergic reaction (FAD – Flea Allergy Dermatitis)

Causes:
- Flea bite saliva causes irritation and allergy
- Common in warm + humid climates

Diagnosis:
- Flea comb exam
- Flea dirt test on white paper
- Skin scraping if secondary infection suspected

Treatment:
- Topical spot-ons (e.g., fipronil, selamectin)
- Oral tablets (nitenpyram, afoxolaner)
- Medicated shampoos
- Treat home environment (bedding, carpets)

Prevention:
- Monthly flea preventives
- Vacuum frequently
- Treat all pets in the household

------------------------------------------------------
2. Ticks
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Visible ticks attached to skin
- Redness at attachment site
- Fever, lethargy (if tick-borne disease)
- Joint pain (Lyme disease)

Causes:
- Outdoor exposure to tick-infested grass
- Common in warm seasons

Diagnosis:
- Visual check
- Blood test if fever develops (Ehrlichia, Babesia, Lyme)

Treatment:
- Remove ticks safely with tweezers
- Tick preventives (fluralaner, amitraz collars)
- Antibiotics for tick-borne diseases

Prevention:
- Anti-tick collars
- Monthly preventive drops
- Avoid tall grass

------------------------------------------------------
3. Mange (Demodectic & Sarcoptic)
------------------------------------------------------

A. Demodectic Mange (“Red Mange”)
---------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Patchy hair loss around face/legs
- Mild itching
- Scaly skin

Cause:
- Overgrowth of Demodex mites in hair follicles

Diagnosis:
- Deep skin scraping

Treatment:
- Ivermectin
- Amitraz dips
- Oral isoxazolines (new effective treatment)

Prevention:
- Strengthen immunity
- Avoid breeding affected dogs

B. Sarcoptic Mange (Scabies)
----------------------------
Symptoms:
- SEVERE itching
- Ear edges affected first
- Crusts, redness, spreading quickly
- Contagious to humans

Diagnosis:
- Skin scraping
- Response to treatment confirms

Treatment:
- Selamectin, Ivermectin, Isoxazolines
- Treat all contact animals

Prevention:
- Avoid contact with infected animals

------------------------------------------------------
4. Ringworm (Dermatophytosis)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Circular hairless patches
- Red ring-like lesions
- Scaling, brittle fur
- More common in kittens/puppies

Cause:
- Fungus (Microsporum, Trichophyton species)
- ZOONOTIC (spreads to humans)

Diagnosis:
- Wood's lamp (glow)
- Fungal culture
- PCR

Treatment:
- Antifungal shampoos (ketoconazole)
- Oral itraconazole or griseofulvin
- Disinfect home environment

Prevention:
- Isolation of infected animal
- Regular cleaning

------------------------------------------------------
5. Allergic Dermatitis (Skin Allergy)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Itchy paws, belly, face
- Redness
- Chronic ear infections
- Hair loss from licking

Causes:
- Food allergy
- Dust mites
- Pollen
- Flea allergy
- Atopy (environmental allergy)

Diagnosis:
- Elimination diet trial
- Intradermal skin test
- Blood allergy test

Treatment:
- Antihistamines
- Immunotherapy
- Omega-3 supplements
- Anti-itch medications (Apoquel, Cytopoint)
- Medicated shampoos

Prevention:
- Identify and avoid triggers
- Maintain flea control

------------------------------------------------------
6. Hot Spots (Acute Moist Dermatitis)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Red, wet, painful patches
- Rapidly enlarging
- Dog constantly licking/chewing area

Causes:
- Moisture trapped under fur
- Allergies
- Fleas
- Ear infections (if on face/neck)

Diagnosis:
- Clinical exam

Treatment:
- Shave area
- Topical antibiotics/steroids
- Cone collar to prevent licking

Prevention:
- Keep coat dry
- Treat underlying allergies

------------------------------------------------------
7. Ear Mites (Otodectes cynotis)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Intense ear scratching
- Brown/black “coffee-ground” ear debris
- Bad smell
- Shaking head frequently

Causes:
- Mites living in ear canal
- Very common in kittens

Diagnosis:
- Ear swab under microscope

Treatment:
- Ear cleaning + mite medication
- Selamectin/ivermectin
- Treat all pets at home

Prevention:
- Regular ear cleaning
- Monthly preventive drops

------------------------------------------------------
8. Pyoderma (Skin Infection)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Pus-filled bumps
- Redness and pain
- Scabs, hair loss
- Common behind legs, belly, chin

Causes:
- Staph bacteria
- Secondary to allergies, fleas, or scratching

Diagnosis:
- Skin cytology
- Culture in chronic cases

Treatment:
- Antibiotics
- Medicated baths (chlorhexidine)
- Treat underlying cause

Prevention:
- Good hygiene
- Control allergies
