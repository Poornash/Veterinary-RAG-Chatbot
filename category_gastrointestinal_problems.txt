========================================
CATEGORY: Gastrointestinal Problems in Dogs & Cats
========================================

This file contains structured, RAG-optimized veterinary knowledge on 
common digestive issues in pets, including symptoms, causes, diagnosis, 
treatment, prevention, and emergency signs.

------------------------------------------------------
1. Vomiting (Acute & Chronic)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Nausea, drooling
- Retching or gagging
- Vomit containing foam, bile, food, grass
- Lethargy, dehydration if severe

Common Causes:
- Dietary indiscretion (ate garbage)
- Food intolerance
- Parasites (roundworms, hookworms)
- Gastritis
- Infections (parvo, distemper)
- Toxins (plants, chemicals, chocolate)
- Foreign body obstruction
- Pancreatitis
- Kidney/Liver disease

Diagnosis:
- Physical exam
- Parvo test (for puppies)
- Blood tests
- X-ray or ultrasound if obstruction suspected

Treatment:
- 6–12 hours fasting (mild cases only)
- Oral rehydration solution (ORS)
- Anti-vomit medication (ondansetron, Cerenia)
- IV fluids for dehydration
- Antibiotics if infection suspected
- Surgery if obstruction present

When Is It an Emergency:
- Blood in vomit
- Vomiting > 24 hours
- Puppy vomiting repeatedly
- Signs of toxicity
- Foreign object eaten (toys, bones)

Prevention:
- Avoid sudden diet changes
- Keep trash out of reach
- Regular deworming

------------------------------------------------------
2. Diarrhea
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Loose/watery stool
- Mucus or blood in stool
- Increased frequency
- Bad smell
- Dehydration

Common Causes:
- Sudden diet change
- Parasites (giardia, coccidia)
- Viral infections (parvo)
- Bacterial infection
- Stress
- Food allergy
- Pancreatitis

Diagnosis:
- Fecal exam for parasites
- Parvo test
- Blood test for dehydration
- Ultrasound if chronic

Treatment:
- Bland diet (boiled chicken + rice)
- Probiotics
- Deworming medication
- Antibiotics (only if bacterial)
- IV fluids for severe dehydration

Emergency Signs:
- Bloody diarrhea
- Puppy/kitten affected
- Continuous diarrhea for >24 hours
- Weakness or collapse

Prevention:
- Keep water clean
- Avoid table scraps
- Vaccination (prevents parvo)
- Deworm regularly

------------------------------------------------------
3. Gastritis (Stomach Inflammation)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Vomiting after eating
- Loss of appetite
- Burping, drooling
- Eating grass
- Abdominal discomfort

Causes:
- Spoiled food
- Eating garbage
- Sudden diet change
- Food allergy
- Bacterial or viral infection

Diagnosis:
- Exam + history
- Rule out obstruction

Treatment:
- Short fasting (not for puppies)
- Bland diet
- Anti-vomit medication
- Antacids (veterinary prescribed)

Prevention:
- Stable diet
- Avoid fatty foods
- Keep chemicals away

------------------------------------------------------
4. Intestinal Worms (Helminths)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Diarrhea
- Vomiting worms
- Bloated belly (especially puppies)
- Weight loss
- Dull coat

Types:
- Roundworms
- Hookworms
- Whipworms
- Tapeworms

Diagnosis:
- Fecal test (microscopy)
- Tapeworm segments seen near anus

Treatment:
- Dewormers (pyrantel, fenbendazole)
- Tapeworm treatment (praziquantel)
- Repeat dose in 2–3 weeks

Prevention:
- Deworm every 3 months
- Keep surroundings clean
- Regular flea control (tapeworms come from fleas)

------------------------------------------------------
5. Foreign Body Obstruction
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Repeated vomiting
- No stool
- Abdominal pain
- Puppy chewing toys
- Drooling
- Restlessness

Common Objects:
- Socks
- Toys
- Bones
- Strings/yarn (especially cats)
- Plastic, metal pieces

Diagnosis:
- Abdominal X-ray
- Ultrasound
- Contrast study

Treatment:
- Endoscopic removal (if in stomach)
- Surgery (if blocked intestine)
- IV fluids + pain meds

Emergency:
- This is ALWAYS an emergency
- No home treatment possible

Prevention:
- Keep small objects away
- Provide safe chew toys

------------------------------------------------------
6. Pancreatitis
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Vomiting
- Abdominal pain (“prayer position”: front down, rear up)
- Diarrhea
- Loss of appetite
- Weakness

Causes:
- Eating oily/fatty foods
- Obesity
- High-fat diet
- Some medications

Diagnosis:
- Blood test: CPL/PLI (Pancreatic Lipase Immunoreactivity)
- Ultrasound

Treatment:
- IV fluids
- Pain relief
- Anti-vomit medication
- Low-fat diet

Prevention:
- Avoid fatty foods
- Maintain healthy weight

------------------------------------------------------
7. Constipation
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Straining to poop
- Hard, dry stool
- Crying during defecation
- Loss of appetite

Causes:
- Low fiber diet
- Dehydration
- Hairball blockage (cats)
- Obesity
- Lack of exercise
- Megacolon (cats)

Diagnosis:
- Rectal exam
- X-ray

Treatment:
- Increase water intake
- Fiber-rich diet
- Lactulose laxative (vet prescribed)
- Enema (done ONLY by vet)

Prevention:
- Hydration
- Regular exercise
- Balanced diet
