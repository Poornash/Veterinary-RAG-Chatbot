===============================
CATEGORY: Infectious Diseases in Pets
===============================

This file contains summarized veterinary knowledge about common infectious diseases in dogs and cats. 
Information includes symptoms, causes, diagnosis, treatment, and prevention.

------------------------------------------------------
1. Canine Parvovirus (Parvo)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Severe bloody diarrhea
- Vomiting
- Fever or low body temperature
- Extreme weakness and dehydration
- Foul-smelling stool

Cause:
- Caused by Canine Parvovirus Type-2
- Spreads through contaminated feces, environment, or infected animals

Diagnosis:
- ELISA fecal test
- CBC shows low WBC count
- PCR test for confirmation

Treatment:
- IV fluids for dehydration
- Anti-vomiting injections
- Antibiotics to prevent secondary infections
- Electrolyte correction
- Plasma transfusion in severe cases

Prevention:
- Vaccination at 6, 8, 12, 16 weeks
- Avoid exposure to infected areas
- Proper disinfection

------------------------------------------------------
2. Canine Distemper
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Fever, watery eyes, nasal discharge
- Coughing
- Vomiting and diarrhea
- Seizures and twitching
- Thickened paw pads ("hard pad disease")

Cause:
- Canine Distemper Virus (CDV)

Diagnosis:
- PCR test
- Blood tests + neurological signs

Treatment:
- Supportive only (no cure)
- IV fluids, antibiotics
- Anti-seizure medications

Prevention:
- Core vaccination (DHP/DHPP)

------------------------------------------------------
3. Kennel Cough (Bordetella)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Dry, hacking cough
- Gagging after coughing
- Runny nose
- Mild fever

Causes:
- Bordetella bronchiseptica bacteria
- Canine Parainfluenza virus
- Highly contagious in boarding kennels

Diagnosis:
- Symptoms + history of exposure
- PCR nasal swab

Treatment:
- Cough suppressants
- Antibiotics if bacterial
- Steam inhalation

Prevention:
- Bordetella vaccine (intranasal/oral)

------------------------------------------------------
4. Leptospirosis
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Sudden fever
- Vomiting, diarrhea
- Jaundice (yellow eyes)
- Kidney failure
- Muscle pain

Cause:
- Leptospira bacteria (spread through rat urine)

Diagnosis:
- MAT test
- PCR test
- Kidney/liver blood work

Treatment:
- Doxycycline antibiotics
- IV fluids
- Liver/kidney support

Prevention:
- Leptospirosis vaccine
- Avoid stagnant water

------------------------------------------------------
5. Rabies
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Sudden behavior changes
- Aggression or anxiety
- Excessive drooling
- Paralysis
- Death (always fatal)

Cause:
- Rabies virus (spread by bite)

Diagnosis:
- Post-mortem brain tissue test
- Symptoms + exposure history

Treatment:
- NONE (100% fatal after symptoms start)

Prevention:
- Mandatory yearly vaccination
- Immediate wound cleaning after bite

------------------------------------------------------
6. Feline Infectious Peritonitis (FIP)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Weight loss
- Fever
- Swollen belly (wet FIP)
- Neurological signs (dry FIP)
- Eye inflammation

Cause:
- Mutation of Feline Coronavirus (FCoV)

Diagnosis:
- Effusion analysis
- PCR
- High protein fluid in abdomen

Treatment:
- Antiviral (GS-441524; available in some countries)
- Supportive care

Prevention:
- Good litter hygiene
- Avoid overcrowding

------------------------------------------------------
7. Feline Upper Respiratory Infection (Cat Flu)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Sneezing
- Runny nose
- Eye discharge
- Mouth ulcers
- Fever

Causes:
- Feline Herpesvirus (FHV)
- Feline Calicivirus (FCV)

Diagnosis:
- Symptoms + PCR

Treatment:
- Antibiotics for secondary infection
- Eye drops
- Steam therapy

Prevention:
- Core FVRCP vaccination
