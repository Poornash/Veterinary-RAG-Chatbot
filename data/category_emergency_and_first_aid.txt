========================================
CATEGORY: Emergency & First Aid for Dogs & Cats
========================================

This file contains structured veterinary emergency guidelines, including 
symptoms, causes, immediate actions, treatment, and when urgent vet care 
is required. Designed to give quick, accurate, life-saving instructions 
(only first-aid, NOT a substitute for professional care).

------------------------------------------------------
1. Heatstroke
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Excessive panting
- Bright red tongue
- Drooling
- Vomiting
- Collapse
- Seizures (severe cases)
- Body temperature > 104°F (40°C)

Common Causes:
- Locked in a hot car
- Vigorous exercise in heat
- Long-haired breeds in summer
- Brachycephalic breeds (pug, bulldog)

Immediate First Aid:
- Move pet to a cool shaded area
- Pour cool (NOT ice-cold) water on body
- Place wet towels on neck, belly, paws
- Offer small amounts of cool water
- Use fan to promote cooling

Do NOT:
- Use ice water
- Cover completely in wet towels (traps heat)
- Force water into mouth

Vet Treatment:
- IV fluids
- Oxygen therapy
- Temperature monitoring
- Stabilizing organ function

Emergency:
- Heatstroke is LIFE-THREATENING → Vet immediately.

------------------------------------------------------
2. Seizures (Fits)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Collapse
- Body stiffening
- Jerking movements
- Drooling, foaming
- Unconsciousness
- Temporary blindness afterward

Causes:
- Epilepsy
- Low blood sugar
- Toxins (chocolate, xylitol)
- Brain infection
- High fever

First Aid:
- Stay calm
- Move objects away to prevent injury
- Do NOT hold the pet down
- Do NOT put anything in the mouth
- Turn off lights/sounds

After Seizure:
- Keep pet warm and calm
- Note duration (important for vet)

Emergency:
- Seizure lasting > 2 minutes
- More than 2 seizures in a day
- First-time seizure in puppy/kitten

------------------------------------------------------
3. Poisoning
------------------------------------------------------
Common Toxins:
- Chocolate
- Xylitol (sugar-free gum)
- Grapes/raisins
- Rat poison
- Onions/garlic
- Paracetamol (cats)
- Household cleaners
- Certain plants (lilies, aloe, dieffenbachia)

Symptoms:
- Vomiting
- Tremors
- Drooling
- Seizures
- Pale/blue gums
- Weakness

First Aid:
- Identify the toxin (label/photo)
- Do NOT induce vomiting unless advised by a vet
- Flush skin with water if chemical exposure
- Keep pet calm

Vet Treatment:
- Activated charcoal
- Antidotes (if toxin known)
- IV fluids
- Seizure control
- Liver/kidney support

Emergency:
- ALL poison cases require immediate vet care.

------------------------------------------------------
4. Bleeding / Injury
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Visible bleeding
- Lameness
- Pain
- Deep cuts
- Bleeding nails

First Aid:
- Apply pressure with clean cloth
- Elevate limb if possible
- Apply bandage (not too tight)
- Styptic powder for nail bleeding

For Large Bleeding:
- Apply firm pressure for 5–10 minutes
- Do NOT remove cloth once blood soaks in (add new layer)

Vet Treatment:
- Sutures or staples
- Antibiotics
- Pain medication

Emergency:
- Spurting arterial blood
- Deep wounds exposing muscle/bone

------------------------------------------------------
5. Fractures (Broken Bones)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Limping or inability to walk
- Swollen limb
- Pain on touch
- Abnormal limb angle

Causes:
- Falls
- Vehicle accidents
- Rough play

First Aid:
- DO NOT try to realign bone
- Keep pet calm and still
- Support limb with towel or cloth
- Carry pet to vet

Vet Treatment:
- X-ray
- Splint or cast
- Surgery (plates/pins)

Emergency:
- Open fracture (bone visible)

------------------------------------------------------
6. Choking
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Gagging
- Pawing at mouth
- Difficulty breathing
- Blue gums

Common Objects:
- Rawhide
- Toys
- Bones
- Balls

First Aid:
- Open mouth and check if object visible
- Remove ONLY if easily reachable
- For dogs: Heimlich-like maneuver (gently lift hips and push beneath ribs)
- For cats: Gentle chest compressions

Vet Treatment:
- Sedation for safe removal
- X-rays
- Treat throat inflammation

Emergency:
- Blue gums = IMMEDIATE action needed

------------------------------------------------------
7. Snake Bite
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Sudden swelling
- Pain at bite site
- Weakness
- Collapse (venomous bite)
- Bleeding from nose/mouth

First Aid:
- Keep pet still
- Do NOT suck venom
- Do NOT apply ice
- Do NOT apply tourniquet
- Take picture of snake if possible (from distance)

Vet Treatment:
- Anti-venom (if needed)
- Pain medication
- IV fluids

Emergency:
- ALL snake bites are emergencies.

------------------------------------------------------
8. Difficulty Breathing (Dyspnea)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Open-mouth breathing (especially cats)
- Blue/pale gums
- Rapid breathing
- Using belly muscles to breathe

Immediate First Aid:
- Keep calm, quiet environment
- Carry (do not allow walking)
- Avoid heat or cold exposure

Emergency:
- ANY breathing difficulty is an emergency.

------------------------------------------------------
9. Eye Injuries
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Squinting
- Rubbing eyes
- Redness
- Cloudy cornea
- Discharge

Causes:
- Foreign body
- Scratch
- Chemical exposure

First Aid:
- Flush eye with saline
- Prevent rubbing (cone collar)

Do NOT:
- Use human eye drops

Emergency:
- Visible wound on eye
- Bulging eye
- Sudden blindness

------------------------------------------------------
10. Urinary Blockage (Mostly in Male Cats)
------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms:
- Straining in litter box
- Crying while urinating
- Frequent attempts with no urine
- Lethargy

Causes:
- Crystals or stones blocking urethra

First Aid:
- NONE at home → LIFE-THREATENING

Vet Treatment:
- Catheterization
- IV fluids
- Pain relief
- Special diet

Emergency:
- Urinary blockage kills within 24–48 hours.
